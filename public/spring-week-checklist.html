Phase 1 of 4
Pre-Spring Week Preparation

The work you do before you arrive is what separates candidates who convert from those who don't.

A
Have the Right Conversations
  • Find 2-3 people who did your exact spring week last year - hearing first-hand experience is the single biggest advantage you can give yourself
  • Ask them how each day went - what tasks, case studies, and presentations they did
  • Ask what they wish they had prepared - what caught them off guard, what they would do differently
  • Ask about the conversion process - was there an AC at the end? Fast-tracked interviews? How many people converted?
  • Ask about the social dynamics - how many interns, how competitive was the vibe, any social events
  • Write down every insight in a preparation document you can review the week before
EarlyEdge Tip
Even one conversation with a previous spring weeker gives you an unfair advantage. Most candidates walk in blind.
B
Build Your Technical Foundations
  • Learn technicals to summer internship level - not spring week level. This is what separates you
  • Don't just memorise formulas - understand application. E.g. know that EV = Equity Value + Net Debt + Minority Interest AND how it appears in real transaction announcements
  • Prepare division-specific knowledge (see the table below for what to focus on)
  • Practice explaining concepts out loud - you need to articulate these in case studies and conversations
  • Work through at least 2 mock case studies relevant to your division
DivisionWhat to Know
Investment BankingValuation methods (DCF, comps, precedent), EV/Equity bridge, M&A transaction mechanics, LBO basics, accounting fundamentals
Markets / S&TBond/equity pricing, derivatives (options, swaps, MBS, CDS), how market factors affect security values, desk functions

Asset ManagementPortfolio allocation, diversification, risk management, hedging strategies, investment philosophies (value, growth, contrarian)

C
Build Awareness and Prepare Questions
  • Read up on the firm's recent transactions and deals - know at least 3 recent pieces of work
  • Understand current market conditions - interest rates, major indices, sector trends, central bank policy
  • Research the teams you'll be meeting - find out which desks/groups you'll shadow (Healthcare, Tech, ECM, DCM, etc.)
  • Prepare 2-3 tailored questions per team - specific to their recent work, not generic "what do you do" questions
  • Track 1-2 live market stories you can reference in conversations (M&A deal, IPO, rate decision, earnings)
  • Research senior people you might meet - LinkedIn profiles, any published articles or deals they led
EarlyEdge Tip
Write your questions in a small notebook. Interviewers notice when you pull out prepared, specific questions. It signals genuine interest.
D
Logistics and Mindset
  • Plan your outfits for every day - dress your smartest, no surprises on the morning
  • Map your commute and arrive 15 minutes early - being late is an instant red flag
  • Prepare your "tell me about yourself" story - 60-90 seconds, covering your background, why finance, and why this firm
  • Have a clear answer for "why this division" - tie it to genuine interest, not just prestige
  • Make sure your LinkedIn is polished and ready to share - clean headshot, updated headline, relevant experience
  • Get proper sleep the 3 nights before - you need to be sharp, not caffeinated and anxious
Common Mistake
Most candidates prepare technicals but forget to prepare their personal story. Senior bankers remember the person, not the valuation answer. Have a compelling narrative about your journey.

Phase 2 of 4
During the Spring Week

HR is watching. Employees are forming opinions. Every interaction counts. Here's how to maximise every day.

A
Daily Non-Negotiables
  • Arrive early, dress sharp, demonstrate attention - basics that many still get wrong
  • Take notes in every session - and review them each evening to refresh for the next day
  • Do not speak over others - take a moderate approach and make your contributions impactful, not frequent
  • Ask at least one thoughtful question per session - use your prepared questions but adapt to what's been discussed
  • Stay for every social event - this is where you build rapport with HR and employees in a relaxed setting
  • Connect with your assigned buddy/shadow - do your research on them beforehand and ask genuine questions
B
Building Relationships
  • Follow up every meaningful conversation with a LinkedIn connection request or email within 24 hours
  • Reference something specific from your conversation in the follow-up message - shows you listened
  • Build strong rapport with HR specifically - they often have significant input on conversion decisions
  • Be genuinely curious about people's careers - ask about their path, not just what their team does
  • Remember names - write them down if you need to. Using someone's name when you see them again is powerful
  • Be kind and collaborative with other spring weekers - competitiveness is noticed negatively
C
Evening Reflection Routine
  • Review your notes from the day's sessions
  • Write down names and key details of everyone you spoke to
  • Draft follow-up messages for LinkedIn/email
  • Prepare questions for tomorrow's sessions based on what you learned today
  • Note anything you want to improve for the next day (spoke too much, too little, missed an opportunity)

Phase 3 of 4
The Conversion Assessment Centre

The format varies by firm: some have ACs on the last day, others a few weeks after, others fast-track you to next year's process. Be ready for all components.

A
Group Case Study
  • Read the instructions independently first - think about your strengths before diving in as a group
  • Volunteer for sections that match your strengths - split the work for efficiency
  • Be critical of information - not everything in the pack is relevant. Time efficiency is key
  • Share findings with the group and discuss critically - challenge ideas constructively
  • Run through the full presentation once as a team before presenting
  • When presenting: stay calm, use notes, support teammates who need help
  • Know the weaknesses in your thesis - assessors will probe them. Anticipate counterarguments
B
Senior Interviews
  • Expect curveballs - "Should we tax charities?", "Do you have what it takes?", "What would you advise our CEO?"
  • Have a compelling personal story - what makes you unique? Why are you here? Sell your journey like a banker sells a deal
  • Give answers that invite follow-up questions - make your responses interesting enough that they want to dig deeper
  • Turn interviews into conversations - ask the MD about their career, their leadership philosophy, what they wish they knew
  • Reference what you learned during the spring week - shows you paid attention and you're already bought in
  • Prepare for standard technicals - valuation, market awareness, accounting, brain teasers (division-dependent)
Questions to Ask Senior Interviewers

"Was there a specific person or moment that shaped your career?"

"What's your personal leadership philosophy?"

"What do you wish you knew when you started, and how would it have changed things?"

C
Maths Test (if applicable)
  • Expect: basic operations, data interpretation, applied finance calculations
  • Practice mental maths daily in the 2 weeks before - percentages, ratios, quick multiplication
  • For markets roles: practice estimation questions - "How long is a train from London to Edinburgh?" Think out loud, explain your reasoning

Phase 4 of 4
After the Spring Week

The spring week ends but the conversion process doesn't. What you do in the days and weeks after matters.

A
Immediate Follow-Up
  • Send thank-you emails to HR, your buddy, and anyone who gave you significant time
  • Connect with every employee you spoke to on LinkedIn with a personalised note
  • Connect with fellow spring weekers - these are your future network in the industry
  • Write a full debrief while it's fresh - what went well, what you'd improve, key learnings
B
Ongoing Relationship Building
  • Keep in touch with your key contacts - send relevant articles, congratulate them on deals, check in quarterly
  • If you don't convert immediately, stay on their radar - politely follow up with HR on next steps
  • Apply what you learned - whether it's technicals, networking skills, or market awareness, keep building
  • Prepare for the full internship application if you're fast-tracked rather than given an immediate offer
