# **Module 1: Why Cold Email Works in Finance**

Most students think breaking into finance means only applying to the big-name banks and going through endless application stages.
But that’s not the only way in.

There’s a whole world of **smaller firms** - hedge funds, family offices, PE boutiques, and prop shops that pay just as well, give you real experience, and don’t make you jump through 10 hoops to get in.

They usually hire through referrals or people already in their network. So yeah they’re probably not “looking” for interns. But that doesn’t mean they won’t *take one on*... if the right person reaches out.

That’s where **cold email** comes in.

Instead of competing with 1,000 other students through a portal, you’re starting a direct conversation with someone who can actually say *“Sure, let’s talk.”*

That message can lead to:
* A remote project
* A paid internship
* A referral or part-time role that was never posted

Most students never try this. Not because they're lazy - they just don't know it's even an option. You’re not sitting around hoping some online portal gets back to you. You’re taking the first step - reaching out to people who actually make the decisions. 

That’s how real opportunities start - one message, one conversation. And those add up, fast.

Let’s start with how to find the right firms and people - quickly, so you’re not wasting time.

# **Module 2: Who to Email (and How to Find Them Fast)**

Before you start reaching out, take a second to ask yourself:
**What areas of finance actually interest me?**

You don’t need a perfect answer. But having a rough idea will help you focus your outreach, sound intentional, and avoid wasting time on firms you don't actually want to work at.

Here’s a list of types of firms where cold outreach tends to work well. These teams are usually lean, move fast, and often don’t advertise internships at all, but they’ll create opportunities when the right person reaches out.

Pick 3 from this list to target first:
* **Asset Managers**
* **Hedge funds**
* **Private equity funds**
* **Search funds**
* **Corporate finance boutiques**
* **M&A advisory firms**
* **Family offices**
* **Real estate investment firms**
* **Venture capital funds**
* **Growth equity funds**
* **Prop trading shops**

Pick the 3 that sound most interesting to you - there’s no “perfect” choice. You can always adjust later.

Once you’ve picked your targets, the next step is to figure out who to contact.
Since these are small firms, your best shot is going straight to senior people. That’s where the decisions get made.

Prioritise:
1. Founders / Managing Partners
2. Managing Directors (MDs)
3. Vice Presidents (VPs)
4. CEOs

*(If you lack contacts, you can also reach out to Associates and Analysts. Tip: if your total number is low, adding other titles like Partner or Director helps generate more leads).*

## **Finding Firms on Apollo.io (Company Search)**

**Learning Objectives:** By the end of this section you will have learnt how to use Apollo.io to find the relevant MDs/CEOs, use keywords to narrow leads, and filter by location. This is the first step in finding REAL people who can help you.

1. First, sign up for [Apollo.io](https://apollo.io). 
2. Go to the **Companies** tab - first we have to filter out by companies to find our MDs/CEOs etc.
3. **Location:** Filter by United Kingdom (or specifically your city, like London, Birmingham, Manchester).
4. **Employees:** Set a custom range of **10 to 30 employees**. Remember, you want to target Small to Medium enterprises (SMEs) which are typically much leaner. This means they do not have a designated HR function, allowing you to reach MDs/CEOs DIRECTLY.
5. **Industry & Keywords:** Think of Industry as your broad category, but rely heavily on Keywords (e.g., “asset management” or “private equity”) to ensure the firm actually mentions that term on their profile. To further refine your search, select any of the segments which apply.
6. Filtering by location allows you to find companies in these areas which fit your criterion - e.g. 8,500 per my example.

# Module 3: Clean Your Leads and Write First Lines

Your exported leads will arrive as a CSV.
It’s time to clean them up so you’re only messaging people at firms that actually matter.

1. Once your leads have been sent over, download the file and open it in Google Sheets/Excel as a .CSV file. Click open files, select the spreadsheet and it should copy in.
2. Cut out every title bar the following: Full name, Email, Linkedin, Website, Cleaned Company Name, and Short Description.
3. Next, add a column for your **First Lines**. Now your data is cleaned we can begin to start writing first lines and collating them.

## The “First Line” Formula

A good first line aims to grab the attention of the reader, conveying genuine enthusiasm. It shows you aren't sending a generic blast. 

**Structure: An Observation + A Simple Question.**

Here's the 2-minute method we use for every single email:

**Check their LinkedIn (30 seconds):**
Scan their headline, their current role, and how long they've been at the firm. Look for a career change, a previous notable role, or how long they've been in the industry.

**Check the Company Short Description (30 seconds):**
Read it. Does the firm do something specific or interesting? Do they specialise in a niche area?

**Check the Company Website (30 seconds):**
Look for a recent project or a mission statement that stands out.

**Write the line (30 seconds):**
Combine what you found into an observation and a question. You only need a few phrases for your first lines, I wouldn’t spend too long on thinking too hard. However, if something you find genuinely intrigues you and you wish to know more write it, but only two sentences max.

**Examples:**
* "I read your recent post about the operational restructuring project last year - how did you cut costs by 30% without reducing headcount?"
* "I saw you recently joined X - how are you finding it so far?"
* "I came across your profile and was inspired by your story/work - what was that like?"
* "I noticed X deals in [Company aim] - how are you navigating in this economic climate?"

Every row on your Outreach sheet should be a relevant contact at a firm you actually want to work at, with a personalised First Line ready to go.

# Module 4: The Cold Email Template

**Learning Objectives:** By the end of this section you will have gone through fundamental principles of what a good template for emails looks like, why they matter, and how to pick apart good templates. This gives us the perfect base to begin writing.

When writing your template it is important to keep in mind, those you outreach to are extremely busy, hence you need to set yourself apart and make what you are asking for and how you can contribute to their firm clear.

Think of your cold email as a conversation starter, not a cover letter. It should be **short, clear, and human** - something the person can read in under 30 seconds.

### **The 5-Part Email Structure**

**1. Subject Line**
Get this wrong and even the best cold email sits unread. The format we recommend: `[Your Name] - [Your University] Cold Email`. Or, I found success initially with: `[UNI] student eager to contribute`.

**2. Personalised First Line & Intro**
Plug in the First Line you wrote in your spreadsheet. Immediately follow it with who you are and what you study.
*Example:* "My name is X and I am at X studying X. I am writing as I am keen to gain paid or unpaid experience at [Company] this summer, even to contribute for a short period."

**3. The Ask**
Don’t say “pick your brain” or "connect". Clearly state you want to gain paid or unpaid experience this summer, even just for a short project. This continues to engage the reader, by not being afraid to put yourself out there which is critical for responses.

**4. Experience & Proof of Initiative (Where you can add value)**
This is where you show the reader HOW your experience relates to your interest within the industry.
*Example:* "My recent internships at [Firm] and [Firm], where I prepared and delivered presentations to stakeholders, boosting operational efficiency by 7% on average, have confirmed my interest in consulting, and I would value the chance to learn from your team."

If you don’t have any experience, or little experience, don’t worry! I would go into more detail about your responsibilities, how you provided a measurable impact and HOW this leads to your interest within your chosen industry.
*Example:* "I’ve been exploring the Industry via societies at [UNI], reading about deals, and building a project where I track and break down interesting funds in Europe. It’s made me realise how much I enjoy thinking like an investor."

Executives don’t look for a polished candidate, but value those with curiosity about the industry and are willing to learn and grow in that environment.
Identify areas where you can provide value, like preparing slide decks, outreach, building financial models etc. This gives the reader a picture of how you could fit within their plans.

**5. Closing & Hyperlinked CV**
This is where you provide information which may be of use to them in coming to a decision, like your CV via a hyperlink.
*Example:* "I have attached my CV for reference, and would love to jump on a call and get to know more about [Company]."

**How to hyperlink your CV:**
1. Upload your CV to google drive as a .pdf and save it as `[Name] CV`.
2. Next, press share and create a link which allows anyone to view it.
3. Paste this shared link into word and then press Control (Command for mac) + K and paste it to hyperlink the text.

# Module 5: Sending Using Outlook Mail Merge

Lets look at a key tenant of your outreach journey - outlook mailmerge. This is how you reach the thousands, ironically the same system used by the automatic rejection emails large corporations use.

We have rigorously tested this. **Why MailMerge and not Gmail?** We tested Mailmerge Vs Google, finding less emails were bounced back and were more secure. Because it is sent from an academic `.ac.uk` domain, it carries credibility and bypasses the stringent automated spam filters that crush random Gmail addresses.

### **Step-by-Step Outlook Mail Merge**

Download your spreadsheet as a CSV file for the first time - we will use a separate sheet once we get going.

1. Go to **Mailings** at the top of Word -> **Start Mail Merge -> Email Messages**. Copy and paste your cold email template here and leave it be for now.
2. Next to it is **Select Recipients -> Use an Existing List**, clicking on the .CSV file you downloaded, pressing ok.
3. Now go to your template and leave spaces for fillers like First name, first line, cleaned company name. This will allow mailmerge to use the spreadsheet to fill in the data.
* `Hi Mr «Last_Name»,`
* `«First_Line»`
* `experience at «Cleaned_Company_Name»`

Note - do not leave spaces on your spreadsheet or copy and paste values which may corrupt the text or create uneven spacing - if you want to edit an email it HAS to be in the spreadsheet inputs you download.

4. **CRITICAL CHECK:** How do I check that individual emails are being written correctly? This is where **"Edit Individual Documents"** on the Finish & Merge icon comes in - a LIFE saver. Always check this before you send your emails to spot typos, grammar issues, and any corrupted text before it goes to a CEO.
5. Once you’re satisfied, click **Merge to Email**. Ensure Outlook is your default mail app for it to automatically start sending.
6. Make sure it is sent as a `HTML message` format (Mandatory, or your formatting will break and it will be all jumbled up together).

Well done, you’ve just done what 99% of the student population are NOT doing.

> [!NOTE]
> **When Should I send my emails?**
> They said ‘the early bird gets the worm’ but don’t be too early - I tried different times at different points in the day so you don't have to. 
> I found the start of the working day (7-8 AM) and the end of the working day (3-5 PM) were not the most optimal for receiving responses. 
> Instead, I found 9-11 AM to be the most optimal, specifically **9:00 AM - 10:00 AM**. 
> Why is this? Most of the time, your contacts will be just logging on and already have their inbox flooded with client emails, junk etc. Sending exactly at **9:03 AM** ensures your email hops to the very top of their inbox *right after* they've just cleared out their morning junk.

### **Pacing Yourself**

For your very first batch, I recommend doing **10-15 emails** to get the hang of it. If you have only scraped around 700-800 leads, I recommend maintaining around 10-20 to focus on the quality of the email.
Once you are confident, scale up to 30-50 per day.

You will have ‘Slow’ days but it is important to be CONSISTENT. This is a numbers game. Note - It is also common out of 50 emails for 2-3 emails to NOT send (bounce back) as domain names scraped may have been from previous companies which are not active. Do not fret, this is perfectly normal.

This strategy helps to bring a good balance between work and studies. I would send my emails, and dive straight back into my uni work for the day, whilst monitoring responses which were coming in. Patience is a virtue. Keep trying and you WILL see results.

# Module 6: Someone actually responded?

Your Mail Merge is out. Now we deal with the responses. 
It is important to distinguish what types of responses you might get.

You will likely receive a typical rejection. But sometimes, MDs will reject you and offer you advice or a 10-minute chat instead. Treat this as a massive win - that is a great opportunity to learn and add to a permanent addition to your network. Say yes to every call.

**What should I respond with if someone gets back to me?**
Aim to get back to them as soon as possible - they will always appreciate punctuality. Thank them for getting back to them even if it is a rejection. If they offer to jump on a call, be as flexible as you can with availability and suggest a time ("Here are times I can make it...") if not suggested already.

### **I have a meeting - what next?**

Congratulations! You are so close to the end.

**1. Be Punctual & Prepared:** Mark it in your calendar. Do your due diligence on a recent deal or their background. Drop a nugget of insight in the first 5 minutes to quickly impress them.

**2. Be Human:** Share the excitement with them without being inauthentic. 95% of these calls are informal chats. Go in open minded, ready to learn and ask questions. Ask them how their day was, and be curious about the work they do.

**3. Be Direct:** When asked to introduce yourself and what you are looking for, do NOT be afraid to set out your aims and desires. You will land a lot of calls which gives you the opportunity to really figure out who you are and what you are looking for. You don't want to spend the summer doing something you hate.

**4. The Follow-Up Email:** If something comes of it great! If not you should still follow-up after the meeting and connect with them on Linkedin.

### **The "Reflection" Habit**

After *every single meeting*, write down a written reflection. What questions did they ask? What did you struggle to answer? What deal did they mention? Why? Reflection helps you to keep track of what you have learnt over your journey, whilst allowing you to take what you have learnt from Meeting 1 into Meeting 40.

Include a piece of this reflection in your post-meeting "Thank You" email:

> *Example: Hi [Name], Just wanted to follow up to say it was great to meet you and to learn more about [COMPANY], especially the work you do in [DEAL]. Let's catch up next week regarding the potential opening.*

### **How do I stay organised with my meetings?**

Once you begin to get a lot of meetings, you will lose track of some, so I recommend having a separate sheet listing the meetings you will have and having them slotted in your calendar to keep an eye on. Being flexible with availability means making it as easy as possible for the MD/CEO on the other end.

# Module 7: How to go above and beyond

### **The Fortune is in the Follow-Up**

Followups are a great way to not only double your response rate, but to show **PERSISTENCE** which MDs/CEOs really value in a new hire. It is important to let go of your ego here, just because someone did not respond right away doesn’t mean they wont respond if nudged appropriately.

Follow-ups can take many forms:
* After a meeting.
* 5 business days (1 week) without a response.
* Following-up on a follow-up, especially if you are in advanced talks with somebody for a role - do NOT be afraid!

You would be surprised what just a small follow-up can generate, a response, a call, an opportunity - the possibilities are endless! That being said, sending more than 2 follow-ups brings marginal gain, so I would stick to 1-2 and move on to the next.

**Pro Tip:** To maintain perfect consistency without burning out, bulk-schedule your follow-ups the night before. Load up two tabs, copy-paste the follow-up text, and schedule them in Outlook to send precisely at **9:03 AM** the next morning. At my peak I was sending 100 emails a day - 50 follow-ups and 50 mailmerges.

### **Meeting 10: What I did after 15 offers**

Lets set the scene - you’ve been at it for 1-2 weeks and landed 5 offers but you want to keep searching to find what’s right for you. In every meeting you’re asked who you are, what are you looking for, and how you can contribute. You notice everytime your answers are different marginally, with refinements making each sound better than the last.

This has a transformative effect on how you conduct yourself - you grow in confidence and as the offers start rolling in you dictate where the conversation goes - asking to take a few days to think about before accepting or declining it.

So by Meeting 10, you are able to use the following factors to gauge whether an offer is right for you:
1. **Will I have enough tangible work to supplement however long I am looking to work?**
2. **Is this opportunity paid? Is there an opportunity to grow my network here?**
3. **Will my work be meaningful, rather than just fetching coffee or running admin tasks?**
4. **Is the company's support network going to enable me to flourish?**

### **Top Tips I used to optimise my outreach**

* **Identifying Tangible Leads:** Flagging deals/leads I could convert and pinning their conversations in Outlook so they were easily accessible.
* **Immediate Scheduling:** As soon as I finished a meeting, writing a follow-up/rejection which I could schedule to send for another date so I would not forget.
* **Reflecting Daily:** Reflecting on the day’s emails and identifying next steps I had to take - this could be preparing for a meeting the following day OR writing out leads.
* **Batching The Workload:** Over time, you will find your motivation will fade which is where your discipline will come into play. Writing 50 first lines felt like it took forever so I began to split it up over hours - 5 first lines at the start of every hour, or 25 in the morning and 25 in the evening to manage the workload. This ensures your approach remains sustainable and you do not become burnt out.
* **Simplifying the Process:** Doing the simplest of things to make my outreach as seamless as possible like loading up 25 emails ready to send on my laptop so the first thing I opened was mailmerge and could press send. Then load up another 25 so within 2 minutes all emails were ready to send. When researching a company, just look for one interesting thing and use any current affairs knowledge to craft a question.
