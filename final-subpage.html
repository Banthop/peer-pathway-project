Module 1

Why Cold Email Works in Finance

Most students think breaking into finance means only applying to the big-name banks and going through endless application stages. But that's not the only way in.

There's a whole world of smaller firms - hedge funds, family offices, PE boutiques, and prop shops - that pay just as well, give you real experience, and don't make you jump through 10 hoops to get in.

They usually hire through referrals or people already in their network. So yeah, they're probably not "looking" for interns. But that doesn't mean they won't take one on… if the right person reaches out.

That's where cold email comes in. Instead of competing with 1,000 other students through a portal, you're starting a direct conversation with someone who can actually say "Sure, let's talk."

That message can lead to:

  • A remote project
  • A paid internship
  • A referral or part-time role that was never posted

Most students never try this. Not because they're lazy - they just don't know it's even an option. But now you do. You're not sitting around hoping some online portal gets back to you. You're taking the first step, reaching out to people who actually make the decisions.

Before you start reaching out, take a second to ask yourself: What areas of finance actually interest me? You don't need a perfect answer. But having a rough idea will help you focus your outreach, sound more intentional, and avoid wasting time on firms you don't actually want to work at.

Once you've picked your targets, figure out who to contact. Since these are small firms, your best shot is going straight to senior people. Prioritise:

  1. Founders / Managing Partners
  2. Managing Directors (MDs)
  3. Vice Presidents (VPs)
  4. CEOs

If you're struggling to find enough contacts at that level, you can also reach out to Associates and Analysts.

Finding Firms on Apollo.io (Company Search)

First, sign up to Apollo.io. Once you're in, go to the Companies tab.

  • Location: United Kingdom (or specifically your city - London, Birmingham, Manchester).
  • Employees: Custom range (3–15 or 10–30). You want to target SMEs which are leaner - they have no designated HR function, letting you reach MDs and CEOs directly.
  • Industry & Keywords: Apollo's industry filter is quite broad. Think of Industry as your rough category, then in Keywords add "asset management" or "private equity" to make sure the firm actually mentions that term.
💡

If you're stuck, ask ChatGPT: "What industries and keywords should I use on Apollo to find small private equity firms in the UK?" You'll get a solid list of inputs to try.

Finding Contacts on Apollo (People Search)

Once you've got your firms, head to the People tab. Filter by Job Title (Managing Partner, MD, VP, CEO). You can also add Associates and Analysts. Then set Contact Location to match your company search (e.g., United Kingdom or London).

Exporting The Smarter Way (ExportApollo)

With Apollo's free plan, you can only manually view around 175 people before it blocks you and asks you to upgrade. Manually copying them works, but it's slow and emails aren't verified.

The smarter way (what we use): ExportApollo is a third-party tool that pulls your entire Apollo search into a clean Google Sheet with verified emails, cleaned company names, and extra data.

  1. Go to exportapollo.com, press Sign Up and head over to the Dashboard. Name your list (e.g., "UK Private Equity 3–15 Staff").
  2. Copy the URL from your Apollo people search and paste it into ExportApollo. Enter the amount of leads to extract. (If it's under 1,000, still enter 1,000 - that's the minimum charge.)
  3. Adjust Settings: Turn ON "Scrape only available emails" (avoids wasting credits), "Email verification" (double-checks emails), "Company Name Cleaning" (removes "LLP" or "Limited"), and "Extra Data".

Click "Pay & Scrape" and you'll receive a Google Sheet delivered to your email. Note: Leads often take 48h to deliver - which gives you perfect time to work on the template.

Clean Your Leads and Write First Lines

Now that you've exported your Apollo leads as a CSV, it's time to clean them up so you're only messaging people at firms that actually matter, with personalised openers that make you stand out.

Download the EarlyEdge Cold Email Tracker. This is where all of your contacts will live from start to finish - clean your leads, track your sends, log responses, and monitor your stats.
[Download the EarlyEdge Cold Email Tracker here]

Open your CSV file. Delete all columns except: First Name, Last Name, Title, Email, LinkedIn Link, Cleaned Company Name, Company Website, Company Full Name, Company Short Description. Copy your CSV data straight into the 'Outreach' sheet of your EarlyEdge Tracker.

The "First Line" Column

You'll see a 'First Line' column already set up in your Outreach sheet. This is where you'll write your personalised opening sentence. Don't spend 10 minutes per contact trying to find the perfect thing to say.

Structure: An Observation + A Simple Question. The goal is to show you've looked them up and you're genuinely curious about learning more.

The 2-minute method:

  • Check their LinkedIn profile (30 seconds). Scan their headline, current role, and look for a career change, previous notable firm, or promotion.
  • Check the Company Short Description (30 seconds). Does the firm do something specific or specialise in a niche area? Have they won an award?
  • Check the Company Website (30 seconds). Scan their homepage or 'About' page. Look for a recent project or mission statement.
  • Write the line (30 seconds). Take whatever you found and turn it into a short observation followed by a simple question.

Fallback templates if you're stuck:

  • "I came across your profile and was inspired by your story, especially how you [interesting thing] - what was that like?"
  • "I saw you've been at [Current Firm] for [X] years now, how's the experience been so far?"
  • "I saw you've moved from [IB/Consulting] into [New Type of Work] - what was that transition like?"
  • "I noticed you went from a larger fund to a boutique - does it feel more hands-on day to day?"
  • "I noticed X deals in [Company aim] - how are you navigating in this economic climate?"

Clean your sheet row by row. Read the Company Description - if the firm isn't relevant, delete the row. Make sure the "Cleaned Company Name" reads naturally (e.g., change "Phoenix Asset Management Partners" to "Phoenix"). Every row in your Outreach sheet should be a relevant contact at a firm you actually want to work at.

Module 4

Write Your Cold Email Template

When writing your template, keep in mind those you outreach to are extremely busy. Think of your cold email as a conversation starter, not a cover letter. It should be short, clear, and human - something the person can read in under 30 seconds.

Your goal is to prove three things: 1) You're genuinely interested in what the firm does, 2) You're eager to learn, 3) You'd be happy to help in any way you can.

Email Template Structure

  1. Subject Line: Get this wrong and even the best cold email sits unread. The format we recommend: [Your Name], [Your University] Cold Email - or [UNI] student eager to contribute. This format consistently gets 3× more opens because it looks like a real email, not a sales pitch.
  2. Personalised First Line: Plug in your First Line observation. Show you've done your research.
  3. Intro + Context: Who you are, what you're studying. Keep it short - 1–2 sentences max.
  4. Why You're Reaching Out (The Ask): Briefly explain why you're messaging them. Make it clear you are seeking paid or unpaid experience this summer, even to contribute for a short period. This is the human touch most AI-driven outreach lacks.
  5. Experience or Proof of Initiative: Show HOW your experience relates. Spring Weeks, society work, or anything relevant - mention it. If you don't have experience, lean into curiosity ("I've been exploring the industry via societies, reading about deals, building a project..."). Identify areas where you can provide value: slide deck prep, market research, Excel models.
  6. Close + CV: Thank them, let them know your CV is attached, and say you're happy to chat briefly.

7 Rules for Writing

  • 1
    Personalise the First Line

    Always start with something specific to the person or firm.

  • 2
    Keep It Short and Skimmable

    120–180 words is the sweet spot. Use line breaks after every 2–3 sentences. Don't cram your CV into the email.

  • 3
    Lead With Humility

    Don't act like you're owed a job. Position it as: "I'm keen to learn, and happy to help wherever I can."

  • 4
    Offer Paid or Unpaid Help

    It shows you're genuinely looking to learn. Most firms will respect this, and many will offer to pay you anyway.

  • 5
    Make Your Ask Clear

    Don't say "I'd love to connect" or "pick your brain." Say: "I'd love to gain experience at your firm this summer."

  • 6
    Attach Your CV (Always)

    Mention that it's attached so they know to look.

  • 7
    Read Your Email Out Loud

    If it sounds robotic, rewrite it. If it sounds like something you'd actually say, it's ready.

Module 5

How to Send Cold Emails Using Outlook Mail Merge

You've done the hard work: built your list, cleaned it, written first lines, and built a solid cold email template. Now it's time to actually send the emails. This module walks you through how to send using Outlook Mail Merge - the cleanest, safest method we recommend for students.

Why Outlook? We've tested this. Outlook gets better reply rates than Gmail for cold emails. Every student has access to Microsoft tools via their university login, and sending from an academic .ac.uk domain bypasses the stringent spam filters that crush random Gmails.

Step-by-Step Outlook Mail Merge

  1. Duplicate and Prepare Your Sheet. Go back to the Google Sheet and make a copy (e.g. "Internship Sending Sheet"). Add a new column called "Sent" and format it as checkboxes. Download this as a CSV file to your desktop.
  2. Start Mail Merge. Open Microsoft Word. Go to Mailings → Start Mail Merge → Email Messages. Click Select Recipients → Use an Existing List and upload your CSV.
  3. Write Template with Placeholders. Paste your draft and use Insert Merge Field for First name, Last name, First Line, Cleaned Company Name. e.g. Hi Mr «Last_Name_», and «First_Line_». Note: do not leave manual spaces in your spreadsheet as this may corrupt uneven spacing.
  4. Add Your CV Link. Upload your CV to Google Drive or OneDrive, set to "Anyone with the link can view". Use Ctrl+K in Word to hyperlink it.

⚠️ Step 5: Critical Check

Click "Edit Individual Documents" on the Finish & Merge icon before sending. This is a lifesaver. Always check this to correct typos, grammar issues, and corrupted text before it goes to a CEO.

Step 6: Finish & Send. Click Merge to Email. Ensure Outlook is your default mail app. Enter your Subject line (e.g. "[Their Name], LSE Cold Email"). Make sure it is sent as HTML message format (mandatory - or your formatting breaks). Hit OK to start sending.

Well done. You've just done what 99% of the student population are NOT doing.

⏰

When to send: Sending 7–8 AM or 3–5 PM is suboptimal. Send exactly at 9:03 AM - it jumps to the top of all the client emails and morning junk they just cleared, standing out in the 9–10 AM window.

Automate Your Outreach with GMass

Sending 30–50 emails daily through Outlook takes time. If you're juggling coursework, GMass automates the entire process directly from your Gmail account.

Important trade-off to understand:

  • Outlook (via school email): Higher reply rates, emails land directly in inboxes because .ac.uk domains have a strong reputation. But it's an intentional, manual process.
  • GMass (via Gmail): Fully automated (set it and forget it, send hundreds while you sleep). But you'll see lower deliverability - Gmail addresses hit spam filters more often.

Our recommendation: Start with Outlook to test your template and get initial replies. Once you've proven what works, consider GMass if you need to scale massively and can accept lower reply rates in exchange for volume. Remember: 10 quality conversations from Outlook beats 100 ignored Gmail emails.

Module 6

What to Do After Sending (Responses & Follow-Ups)

Now it's about consistency. After you send, go back to your Google Sheet and tick the checkbox under the "Sent" column. Keep a consistent schedule: send 30 cold emails per day minimum. If deliverability is good, scale it to 50 a day. Send Monday to Friday (avoid weekends). Aim for a consistent time (9:03 AM). Note: it's very common for 2–3 out of 50 emails to bounce back - this is perfectly normal.

You will likely receive typical rejections. But sometimes, MDs will reject you and offer advice or a 10-minute chat instead. Treat this as a massive win - say YES to every call.

The Follow-Up Method

The fortune is in the follow-up. It shows persistence, which MDs respect. Schedule follow-ups the night before to be sent at exactly 9:03 AM the next day. If you don't get a reply after 5–7 days, send one polite follow-up nudge.

When to follow up: Create a 'Followed up?' tab and enter the date. Example: an email sent Monday 1st January would need to be followed up on Monday January 8th. As you progress, tighten this to 4 business days (e.g., email sent Monday January 22nd → followed up Friday 26th). At peak I was sending 100 emails a day: 50 follow-ups and 50 mail-merges.

✉️

Example nudge: "Hi [Name], Just wanted to follow up on my previous email in case it got lost in your inbox. I'd still love the chance to learn more about your work at [Firm Name] and help however I can this summer. Thanks again, [Your Name]."

I Have a Meeting - What Next?

  1. Reply immediately. Aim to reply within the first 10 minutes. Suggest times to make it frictionless for them. Mark it in your calendar.
  2. Be Punctual & Prepared. Do your due diligence on a recent deal or their background. Drop a nugget of insight in the first 5 minutes to quickly impress them.
  3. Be Human. Share the excitement without being inauthentic. 95% of these calls are informal chats. Go in open-minded, ready to learn and ask questions. Ask how their day was, and be curious about the work they do.
  4. Be Direct. When asked to introduce yourself and what you're looking for, don't be afraid to set out your aims and desires. You don't want to spend the summer doing something you hate.
  5. Follow-Up Email. Write a reflection after every call. Note what they asked and deals they mentioned. Include this detail in your post-meeting "Thank You" email, and make sure to connect with them on LinkedIn.

Meeting 10: What I did after 15 offers

Let's set the scene: you've been at it for 1–2 weeks and landed 5 offers but you want to keep searching to find what's right for you. Your answers get refined with each call and your confidence grows. You dictate where the conversation goes, asking to take a few days to think before accepting or declining.

Gauge your offers based on 4 factors:

  • A) Will I have actual tangible work?
  • B) Is it paid and can I grow my network?
  • C) Is the work meaningful or just repetitive admin?
  • D) Is their support framework going to help me flourish?

Top Tips to Optimise Your Outreach

  • Identifying Tangible Leads: Flag deals and leads you could convert, and pin their conversations in Outlook so they're easily accessible.
  • Immediate Scheduling: As soon as you finish a meeting, write a follow-up or rejection email and schedule it for another date so you won't forget.
  • Reflecting Daily: Reflect on the day's emails and identify next steps you need to take.
  • Batching the Workload: Over time your motivation will fade, so relying on this exact system is critical. Writing 50 first lines felt like it took forever - split it up: 5 first lines at the start of every hour, or 25 in the morning and 25 in the evening to manage the workload and prevent burnout.
  • Simplifying the Process: Do the simplest things to make your outreach seamless. Load up 25 emails ready to send on your laptop so the first thing you open is mail merge and you can press send immediately.
