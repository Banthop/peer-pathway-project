Module 1: Why Cold Email Works in Finance

Most students think breaking into finance means only applying to the big-name banks and going through endless application stages.

But that’s not the only way in.

There’s a whole world of smaller firms - hedge funds, family offices, PE boutiques, and prop shops that pay just as well, give you real experience, and don’t make you jump through 10 hoops to get in.

They usually hire through referrals or people already in their network.

So yeah they’re probably not “looking” for interns.

But that doesn’t mean they won’t take one on... if the right person reaches out.

That’s where **cold email** comes in.

Instead of competing with 1,000 other students through a portal, you’re starting a direct conversation with someone who can actually say “Sure, let’s talk.”

That message can lead to:

  • A remote project
  • A paid internship
  • A referral or part-time role that was never posted

Most students never try this.

Not because they're lazy - they just don't know it's even an option.

You’re not sitting around hoping some online portal gets back to you.

You’re taking the first step - reaching out to people who actually make the decisions.

That’s how real opportunities start - one message, one conversation.

And those add up, fast.

Let’s start with how to find the right firms and people - quickly, so you’re not wasting time.

Module 2: Who to Email (and How to Find Them Fast)

Before you start reaching out, take a second to ask yourself:

What areas of finance actually interest me?

You don’t need a perfect answer.

But having a rough idea will help you focus your outreach, sound intentional, and avoid wasting time on firms you don't actually want to work at.

Here’s a list of types of firms where cold outreach tends to work well.

These teams are usually lean, move fast, and often don’t advertise internships at all, but they’ll create opportunities when the right person reaches out.

Pick 3 from this list to target first:

  • Asset Managers
  • Hedge funds
  • Private equity funds
  • Search funds
  • Corporate finance boutiques
  • M&A advisory firms
  • Family offices
  • Real estate investment firms
  • Venture capital funds
  • Growth equity funds
  • Prop trading shops

Pick the 3 that sound most interesting to you - there’s no “perfect” choice.

You can always adjust later.

Once you’ve picked your targets, the next step is to figure out who to contact.

Since these are small firms, your best shot is going straight to senior people.

That’s where the decisions get made.

Prioritise:

  1. Founders / Managing Partners
  2. Managing Directors (MDs)
  3. Vice Presidents (VPs)
  4. CEOs

(If you lack contacts, you can also reach out to Associates and Analysts).

Finding Firms on Apollo.io (Company Search)

First, sign up for Apollo.io.

Go to the Companies tab - this is where you'll start your search.

Location: Filter by United Kingdom (or specifically your city, like London).

Employees: Set a custom range of 10 to 30 employees.

Small to Medium enterprises (SMEs) are much leaner, lacking a thick HR department, which allows you to reach MDs and CEOs DIRECTLY.

Industry & Keywords: Think of Industry as your broad category (e.g., “Investment Management” or "Financial Services"), but rely heavily on Keywords (e.g., “asset management” or “private equity”) to ensure the firm actually mentions that term on their profile.

Tip: if you’re stuck, use ChatGPT. Ask it: "What industries and keywords should I use on Apollo to find small private equity firms in the UK?"

Finding Contacts on Apollo.io (People Search)

Once you’ve got your firms, head to the People tab.

This is where you'll start building your list of the decision-makers you'll be emailing.

Start by filtering by Job Title.

Add titles like "Managing Partner, Managing Director, CEO, Vice President."

These are the senior people most likely to say yes to a conversation.

Set the Contact Location to match your target area (e.g. United Kingdom or London).

Exporting The Smart Way: ExportApollo

We use a third-party tool called ExportApollo.com to pull your entire Apollo search into a clean Google Sheet with verified emails for just $8 per 1,000 contacts.

Module 3: Clean Your Leads and Write First Lines

Your exported leads will arrive as a CSV.

It’s time to clean them up so you’re only messaging people at firms that actually matter.

Open a new Google Sheet and import your CSV file.

Delete all columns except: First Name, Last Name, Title, Email, LinkedIn Link, Cleaned Company Name, Company Website, and Company Short Description.

Add a new column called "First Line".

The “First Line” Formula

Your First Line is your personalised opening sentence.

It shows you aren't sending a generic blast.

Structure: An Observation + A Simple Question.

The goal is to show you’ve looked them up, and you're actually curious about learning more about them/their firm.

Here's the 2-minute method we use for every single email:

Check their LinkedIn (30 seconds):
Scan their headline, their current role, and how long they've been at the firm. Look for a career change, a previous notable role, or how long they've been in the industry.

Check the Company Short Description (30 seconds):
Read it. Does the firm do something specific or interesting? Do they specialise in a niche area?

Check the Company Website (30 seconds):
Look for a recent project or a mission statement that stands out.

Write the line (30 seconds):
Combine what you found into an observation and a question.

(Example: "I read your recent post about the operational restructuring project last year - how did you cut costs by 30% without reducing headcount?")

If you’re struggling to find something personal, here are a few fallback lines you can use:

  • I came across your firm while researching smaller [Type of Firm] - what’s it like working in a smaller team environment?
  • I saw you joined [Current Firm] not too long ago - what drew you to the team?
  • I noticed you went from a larger fund to a boutique - does it feel more hands-on day-to-day?
  • I saw you’ve moved from [Investment Banking/Consulting/etc.] into [New Type of Work] - what was that transition like?

Now, read the firm's description.

If they aren't relevant, delete the row entirely.

Every row on your Outreach sheet should be a relevant contact at a firm you actually want to work at, with a personalised First Line ready to go.

Module 4: The Cold Email Template

This is the most important part of the process.

Everything you’ve done so far - building your list, cleaning it, and writing your first lines has been preparing for this.

Your goal with this email is to prove three things:

  1. You’re genuinely interested in what the firm does
  2. You’re eager to learn and gain real experience
  3. You’d be happy to help in any way you can

When done right, it doesn’t come across as desperate or transactional.

It shows maturity, initiative, and curiosity - exactly what smaller firms look for in students.

Think of your cold email as a conversation starter, not a cover letter.

It should be short, clear, and human - something the person can read in under 30 seconds.

The 5-Part Email Structure

1. Subject Line
Get this wrong and even the best cold email sits unread. The format we recommend: [Your Name] - [Your University] Cold Email.
Why? It looks like a real email, not a sales pitch. It tells them who you are but not exactly what you want - curiosity always beats information.

2. Personalised First Line & Intro
Plug in the First Line you wrote in your spreadsheet. Immediately follow it with who you are and what you study.

3. The Ask
Don’t say “pick your brain” or "connect". Clearly state you want to gain paid or unpaid experience this summer, even just for a short project.

4. Experience & Proof of Initiative
Show how your experience relates to your interest. Don't have formal experience? Lean into what you've done in university societies or personal projects. Clearly identify exactly how you can help them (e.g., "I can help with market research, slide deck preparation, or outreach"). Executives don’t look for polished candidates; they look for curiosity and a willingness to learn.

5. Closing & Hyperlinked CV
Thank them, mention your CV is attached, and say you're happy to chat.
Do NOT paste your CV in the body, and avoid raw file attachments that trigger spam filters. Hyperlink it instead.

How to Hyperlink Your CV Properly:

  1. Upload your CV to Google Drive as a PDF (Name it: [Your Name] CV).
  2. Set the sharing permissions to "Anyone with the link can view".
  3. In Word/Outlook, type "I've attached my CV for reference", highlight the text, hit Ctrl+K (Cmd+K on Mac), and paste the link.

Example Template:

Subject: Tony Walker - LSE Cold Email

Hi Mr Allen,

I saw you joined ABC Associates not too long ago - what drew you to the team?

My name is Tony Walker, and I’m a first-year BSc Finance student at the London School of Economics.

I’m reaching out to express interest in getting unpaid or paid experience this summer at ABC Associates - even just a short project would be a great chance to learn.

My recent internships at Nomura and J.P. Morgan, where I delivered presentations to stakeholders boosting operational efficiency by 7%, have confirmed my interest in advisory work.

I would truly value the chance to learn from your team.

I would be happy to help with market research, slide deck preparation, or any analytical tasks.

I’ve [linked my CV here] for reference, and would love to jump on a quick call to get to know more about ABC Associates.

Best regards,

Tony Walker

Module 5: Sending Using Outlook Mail Merge

We have rigorously tested this.

Outlook gets drastically better reply rates than Gmail for cold emails.

Because it is sent from an academic .ac.uk domain, it carries credibility and bypasses the stringent automated spam filters that crush random Gmail addresses.

Step-by-Step Outlook Mail Merge

Go back to your Google Sheet and download it as a **CSV**.

Open a blank Microsoft Word document.

Go to Mailings -> Start Mail Merge -> Email Messages.

Click Select Recipients -> Use an Existing List and upload your CSV.

Copy and paste your cold email template.

Use Insert Merge Field to swap out your placeholders:

  • Hi Mr «Last_Name»,
  • «First_Line»
  • experience at «Cleaned_Company_Name»

CRITICAL CHECK: Click "Edit Individual Documents" on the Finish & Merge icon before hitting send!

This lets you review the generated emails to spot typos, grammar issues, or corrupted formatting before it goes to a CEO.

Once satisfied, click Finish & Merge -> Send Email Messages.

  • To: Email
  • Subject: [Your Name] - [Your University] Cold Email
  • Mail format: HTML (Mandatory, or your formatting will break).

The 9:03 AM Rule

When should you send? We tried different times at different points in the day so you don't have to.

7-8 AM and 3-5 PM get completely buried.

The absolute most optimal time for responses is 9:00 AM - 10:00 AM.

Sending exactly at 9:03 AM ensures your email hops to the very top of their inbox right after they've just cleared out their morning junk and client overnight emails.

Pacing Yourself

For your very first batch, just send 10-15 emails.

Once you are confident the Mail Merge worked without visual errors, scale up to 30-50 per day.

It is perfectly normal for 2-3 emails out of a batch of 50 to "bounce back."

Do not panic - this just means that executive recently left the firm.

Module 6: Managing Responses & Following Up

Your Mail Merge is out.

Now we deal with the responses.

You will likely receive a typical rejection.

But sometimes, MDs will reject you and offer you advice or a 10-minute chat instead.

Treat this as a massive win - that is a permanent addition to your network.

Say yes to every call.

The Fortune is in the Follow-Up

A simple follow-up will literally double your response rate.

It shows persistence - a trait MDs actively test for.

If they haven't responded after 5 business days (1 week), send a gentle nudge:

"Hi [Name], Just wanted to follow up on my previous email in case it got lost in your inbox. I’d still love the chance to learn more about your work at [Firm Name] and help however I can this summer. Thanks, [Your Name]."

Pro Tip: To maintain perfect consistency without burning out, bulk-schedule your follow-ups the night before. Load up two tabs, copy-paste the follow-up text, and schedule them in Outlook to send precisely at 9:03 AM the next morning.

Securing the Meeting & Getting the Offer

When they offer to jump on a call, reply immediately (within the first 10 minutes if possible).

Suggest times to make it frictionless for them.

1. Be Punctual & Prepared: Do your due diligence on a recent deal or their background. Drop a nugget of insight in the first 5 minutes.

2. Be Human: 95% of these calls are informal chats. Go in open minded. Ask them how their day was. Share genuine excitement.

3. Be Direct: When asked what you are looking for, do not be afraid to set out your aims cleanly. You don't want to spend the summer doing something you hate.

The "Reflection" Habit

After every single meeting, write down a written reflection.

What questions did they ask? What did you struggle to answer? What deal did they mention?

This reflection helps you iterate, meaning you take the lessons from Meeting 1 and apply them to crush Meeting 40.

Include a piece of this reflection in your post-meeting "Thank You" email:

"Hi [Name], Great to meet you earlier. I particularly found your insights on the mid-market SaaS structuring really fascinating. Let's catch up next week regarding the potential opening."

Handling 15+ Offers

If you stay disciplined, sending 50 emails a day with the 9:03 AM strategy, you are going to land multiple offers.

Every new email sent and meeting had is a chance to learn and meet new people.

As your confidence grows, your tone on the calls will change. You will begin to dictate the conversation.

Use these 4 criteria to decide what offer to accept:

  1. Will I have enough tangible work?
  2. Is there opportunity to grow my network here?
  3. Will my work be meaningful, rather than just fetching coffee or running admin tasks?
  4. Is the company's support network going to enable me to flourish?
